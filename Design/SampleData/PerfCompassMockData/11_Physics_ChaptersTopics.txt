**Chapter 8: MECHANICAL PROPERTIES OF SOLIDS**
*   **Stress and Strain:** Understanding the restoring force per unit area (stress) and the fractional change in dimension (strain) when a body is deformed.
*   **Hooke’s Law:** The proportional relationship between stress and strain for small deformations.
*   **Stress-Strain Curve:** Analyzing the relationship between stress and strain for materials to understand properties like yield point and tensile strength.
*   **Elastic Moduli:**
    *   **Young’s Modulus:** Ratio of tensile/compressive stress to longitudinal strain.
    *   **Shear Modulus:** Ratio of shearing stress to shearing strain.
    *   **Bulk Modulus:** Ratio of hydraulic stress to volume strain.
*   **Applications of Elastic Behaviour:** Practical uses in engineering, such as the design of beams, bridges, and cranes.

**Chapter 9: MECHANICAL PROPERTIES OF FLUIDS**
*   **Pressure:** Force per unit area exerted by a fluid, including Pascal's Law regarding the transmission of pressure in a fluid at rest.
*   **Streamline Flow:** The study of fluids in motion (fluid dynamics) where velocity at a point remains constant in time.
*   **Bernoulli’s Principle:** The conservation of energy in fluid flow, relating pressure, kinetic energy, and potential energy.
*   **Viscosity:** The internal friction or resistance to motion between layers of a fluid.
*   **Surface Tension:** The property of liquid surfaces to shrink into the minimum surface area possible, caused by cohesive forces between molecules.

**Chapter 10: THERMAL PROPERTIES OF MATTER**
*   **Temperature and Heat:** Defining temperature as a measure of hotness and heat as the energy transferred due to temperature difference.
*   **Measurement of Temperature:** Principles of thermometers and temperature scales like Celsius, Fahrenheit, and Kelvin.
*   **Ideal-Gas Equation:** The relationship $PV = \mu RT$ connecting pressure, volume, and temperature.
*   **Thermal Expansion:** The increase in dimensions (linear, area, volume) of a body due to an increase in temperature.
*   **Specific Heat Capacity:** The amount of heat required to raise the temperature of a unit mass of substance by one degree.
*   **Calorimetry:** The measurement of heat transfer.
*   **Change of State:** Transitions between solid, liquid, and gas phases, including concepts like latent heat.
*   **Heat Transfer:** The three modes of transfer: Conduction, Convection, and Radiation.
*   **Newton’s Law of Cooling:** The rate of heat loss of a body is proportional to the temperature difference between the body and its surroundings.

**Chapter 11: THERMODYNAMICS**
*   **Thermal Equilibrium:** The state where macroscopic variables do not change, leading to the Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics.
*   **First Law of Thermodynamics:** The law of conservation of energy ($\Delta Q = \Delta U + \Delta W$) applied to thermodynamic systems.
*   **Specific Heat Capacity:** Molar specific heats at constant pressure ($C_p$) and constant volume ($C_v$).
*   **Thermodynamic Processes:** Types of processes including Isothermal, Adiabatic, Isochoric, and Isobaric.
*   **Second Law of Thermodynamics:** Limits on the efficiency of heat engines and the direction of heat transfer.
*   **Reversible and Irreversible Processes:** Distinguishing between ideal reversible changes and natural irreversible ones.
*   **Carnot Engine:** An ideal reversible heat engine operating between two temperatures.

**Chapter 12: KINETIC THEORY**
*   **Molecular Nature of Matter:** The atomic hypothesis and the behavior of molecules in solids, liquids, and gases.
*   **Behaviour of Gases:** Gas laws (Boyle's, Charles') and the ideal gas equation derived from molecular theory.
*   **Kinetic Theory of an Ideal Gas:** Interpreting pressure and temperature in terms of molecular motion and kinetic energy.
*   **Law of Equipartition of Energy:** The distribution of energy equally among all degrees of freedom.
*   **Specific Heat Capacity:** Theoretical predictions of specific heats for monoatomic, diatomic, and polyatomic gases.
*   **Mean Free Path:** The average distance a molecule travels between collisions.

**Chapter 13: OSCILLATIONS**
*   **Periodic and Oscillatory Motions:** Defining motion that repeats at regular intervals.
*   **Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM):** Oscillatory motion where restoring force is proportional to displacement.
*   **SHM and Uniform Circular Motion:** Visualizing SHM as the projection of circular motion.
*   **Velocity and Acceleration in SHM:** The mathematical relationships of velocity and acceleration to displacement and time.
*   **Force Law for SHM:** The linear relationship $F = -kx$.
*   **Energy in SHM:** The interchange and conservation of kinetic and potential energy during oscillation.
*   **The Simple Pendulum:** Analysis of a small bob oscillating on a string as an SHM system.

**Chapter 14: WAVES**
*   **Transverse and Longitudinal Waves:** Distinguishing waves by the direction of oscillation relative to propagation.
*   **Displacement Relation in a Progressive Wave:** Mathematical description of a travelling wave.
*   **Speed of a Travelling Wave:** Determining wave speed based on medium properties (e.g., tension in a string).
*   **Principle of Superposition of Waves:** How overlapping waves combine algebraically.
*   **Reflection of Waves:** Behavior of waves at rigid and open boundaries.
*   **Beats:** The phenomenon arising from the interference of waves with slightly different frequencies.
