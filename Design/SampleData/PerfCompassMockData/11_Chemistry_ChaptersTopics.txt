**Unit 1: Some Basic Concepts of Chemistry**
*   **Importance of Chemistry:** The role of chemistry in science and its applications in daily life, such as in agriculture, health, and industry.
*   **Nature of Matter:** Classification of matter into solids, liquids, and gases; elements, compounds, and mixtures.
*   **Properties of Matter and their Measurement:** Physical and chemical properties; The International System of Units (SI); Mass and Weight; Volume; Density; Temperature.
*   **Uncertainty in Measurement:** Scientific notation; Significant figures; Precision and Accuracy; Dimensional Analysis.
*   **Laws of Chemical Combinations:** Law of Conservation of Mass; Law of Definite Proportions; Law of Multiple Proportions; Gay Lussac’s Law of Gaseous Volumes; Avogadro’s Law.
*   **Dalton’s Atomic Theory:** The concept of atoms as indivisible building blocks of matter.
*   **Atomic and Molecular Masses:** Atomic mass; Average atomic mass; Molecular mass; Formula mass.
*   **Mole Concept and Molar Masses:** The definition of the mole; Avogadro constant; Molar mass.
*   **Percentage Composition:** Mass percentage of elements; Empirical formula; Molecular formula.
*   **Stoichiometry and Stoichiometric Calculations:** Balancing chemical equations; Limiting reagent; Reactions in solutions (Mass percent, Mole fraction, Molarity, Molality).

**Unit 2: Structure of Atom**
*   **Discovery of Sub-atomic Particles:** Discovery of electron, proton, and neutron; Charge to mass ratio of electron; Charge on electron.
*   **Atomic Models:** Thomson model of atom; Rutherford’s nuclear model of atom; Atomic number and mass number; Isobars and Isotopes.
*   **Developments Leading to the Bohr’s Model of Atom:** Wave nature of electromagnetic radiation; Particle nature of electromagnetic radiation (Planck’s Quantum theory, Photoelectric effect); Atomic spectra (Line spectrum of hydrogen).
*   **Bohr’s Model for Hydrogen Atom:** Postulates; Explanation of line spectrum of hydrogen; Limitations of Bohr’s model.
*   **Towards Quantum Mechanical Model of the Atom:** Dual behaviour of matter (de Broglie); Heisenberg’s Uncertainty Principle.
*   **Quantum Mechanical Model of Atom:** Schrödinger equation (brief concept); Orbitals and Quantum numbers (Principal, Azimuthal, Magnetic, Spin); Shapes of atomic orbitals; Energies of orbitals; Filling of orbitals in atoms (Aufbau Principle, Pauli Exclusion Principle, Hund’s Rule); Electronic Configuration of atoms.

**Unit 3: Classification of Elements and Periodicity in Properties**
*   **Genesis of Periodic Classification:** Historical development (Dobereiner's Triads, Newlands' Octaves, Mendeleev’s Periodic Table).
*   **Modern Periodic Law:** Atomic number as the basis; Present form of the Periodic Table (Periods and Groups).
*   **Nomenclature of Elements with Atomic Numbers > 100:** IUPAC systematic nomenclature.
*   **Electronic Configurations and the Periodic Table:** Electronic configurations in periods and groups.
*   **Electronic Configurations and Types of Elements:** Characteristics of s-, p-, d-, and f-Block elements; Metals, Non-metals, and Metalloids.
*   **Periodic Trends in Properties of Elements:** Trends in physical properties (Atomic Radius, Ionic Radius, Ionization Enthalpy, Electron Gain Enthalpy, Electronegativity); Periodic trends in chemical properties (Periodicity of Valence/Oxidation States, Anomalous properties of second period elements).

**Unit 4: Chemical Bonding and Molecular Structure**
*   **Kössel-Lewis Approach to Chemical Bonding:** Octet Rule; Covalent Bond; Lewis Representation; Formal Charge; Limitations of the Octet Rule.
*   **Ionic or Electrovalent Bond:** Formation of ionic bonds; Lattice Enthalpy.
*   **Bond Parameters:** Bond Length, Bond Angle, Bond Enthalpy, Bond Order, Resonance Structures, Polarity of Bonds (Dipole Moment).
*   **The Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion (VSEPR) Theory:** Predicting geometry of molecules.
*   **Valence Bond Theory:** Orbital Overlap Concept; Directional properties of bonds; Overlapping of Atomic Orbitals; Types of overlapping (Sigma and Pi bonds).
*   **Hybridisation:** Types of hybridisation (sp, sp2, sp3, involving d orbitals).
*   **Molecular Orbital Theory:** Formation of Molecular Orbitals (LCAO); Energy Level Diagram; Electronic Configuration and Molecular Behaviour; Bonding in Homonuclear Diatomic Molecules.
*   **Hydrogen Bonding:** Cause of formation; Types of H-Bonds.

**Unit 5: Thermodynamics**
*   **Thermodynamic Terms:** System and Surroundings; Types of System (Open, Closed, Isolated); State of the System; Internal Energy as a State Function; Work; Heat.
*   **First Law of Thermodynamics:** Mathematical statement; Applications (Work, Enthalpy).
*   **Measurement of ∆U and ∆H:** Calorimetry.
*   **Enthalpy Change (∆rH) of a Reaction:** Standard Enthalpy of Reactions; Enthalpy Changes during Phase Transformations; Standard Enthalpy of Formation; Thermochemical Equations; Hess’s Law of Constant Heat Summation.
*   **Enthalpies for Different Types of Reactions:** Enthalpy of Combustion, Atomization, Bond Enthalpy, Lattice Enthalpy, Solution, Dilution.
*   **Spontaneity:** Decrease in Enthalpy; Entropy and Spontaneity; Gibbs Energy and Spontaneity; Second and Third Laws of Thermodynamics.
*   **Gibbs Energy Change and Equilibrium:** Relationship between free energy change and equilibrium constant.

**Unit 6: Equilibrium**
*   **Equilibrium in Physical Processes:** Solid-liquid, Liquid-vapour, Solid-vapour equilibria; Dissolution of solids/gases in liquids.
*   **Equilibrium in Chemical Processes:** Dynamic nature of chemical equilibrium.
*   **Law of Chemical Equilibrium and Equilibrium Constant:** Law of Mass Action; Relation between Kp and Kc.
*   **Homogeneous and Heterogeneous Equilibria:** Equilibrium constants for gaseous systems and multi-phase systems.
*   **Applications of Equilibrium Constants:** Predicting extent and direction of reaction; Calculating equilibrium concentrations.
*   **Relationship between Equilibrium Constant K, Reaction Quotient Q, and Gibbs Energy G:**.
*   **Factors Affecting Equilibria:** Le Chatelier’s Principle (Effect of Concentration, Pressure, Inert Gas, Temperature, Catalyst).
*   **Ionic Equilibrium in Solution:** Strong and weak electrolytes.
*   **Acids, Bases and Salts:** Arrhenius, Brönsted-Lowry, and Lewis concepts.
*   **Ionization of Acids and Bases:** Ionization Constants of Weak Acids and Bases; Ionization of Water (Kw); pH Scale; Relation between Ka and Kb; Di- and Polybasic Acids; Common Ion Effect; Hydrolysis of Salts.
*   **Buffer Solutions:** Preparation and designing buffer solutions.
*   **Solubility Equilibria of Sparingly Soluble Salts:** Solubility Product Constant (Ksp); Common Ion Effect on Solubility.

*   **Oxidation Number:** Concepts and applications.
*   **Electrode Processes:** Redox reactions and electrode processes.

### **Unit 8: Organic Chemistry – Some Basic Principles and Techniques**
*   **Tetravalence of Carbon:** Shapes of organic compounds and hybridization.
*   **Structural Representations:** Complete, condensed, bond-line formulas, and 3D representations.
*   **Classification:** Acyclic, cyclic, aromatic compounds, functional groups, and homologous series.
*   **Nomenclature:** IUPAC system for alkanes and substituted benzene compounds.
*   **Isomerism:** Structural isomerism (chain, position, functional, metamerism) and stereoisomerism.
*   **Reaction Mechanism Concepts:**
    *   Fission of covalent bonds (homolytic and heterolytic).
    *   Nucleophiles and Electrophiles.
    *   Electron movement and displacement effects (Inductive, Resonance, Electromeric, Hyperconjugation).
*   **Methods of Purification:** Sublimation, crystallization, distillation, differential extraction, and chromatography.
*   **Qualitative Analysis:** Detection of Carbon, Hydrogen, Nitrogen, Sulphur, Halogens, and Phosphorus.
*   **Quantitative Analysis:** Estimation of elements including Carbon, Hydrogen, Nitrogen, Halogens, Sulphur, Phosphorus, and Oxygen.
