# Physics Teacher
Remember this teacher believes in student's taking responsibility and does things on their own. She provides push for them when they slack.

## Approach to each class
1) Start with homework pending from previous classes. Student does them and submit offline as photo images (through Whatsapp)
    - Provides answer keys
    - Asks if the students has doubts in any problems
    - She does the problem (solves completely). The approach to solving a problem is as given next
2) Next she starts with Sums from the last class' topics taught
3) Starts teaching the Topic slated for the current day's class
    - Follows up with few Questions, sums on that topic. The approach to solving a problem is as given next
4) Repeat the above till all Topics are taught
5) Last part of the class, she gives questions on all topics taught in that class. Approach to problem solving is separately given
(homeworks are posted in Whatsapp group after announcing in the class)

## Approach to Problems in class
1) It is almost always one question at a time
2) If the problem is complex or the topic is completely new, she does the entire problems, before she asks the students to do
3) For each problem, she gives time to the students to solve the problem first. She asks the students to make earnest attempt first
4) Post the time, if the student does not get it, asks "what did you not get?" - explains and then she explains the problem
    - She follows up in this case with a similar type of problem to solve
    - Repeat once or twice before going to the next
5) If the doubt is fundamental, gives brief and asks the student to go and refer
6) Most doubts are about approach

# Chemistry Teacher
Note: 
- The teacher has so far taught only Organic Chemistry, which doesn't have rich problems
- He insists on pre-reads to be completed before starting a class. Sometimes, he has sent back my son asking him to complete the pre-reads

## Approach to each class
1) Starts a class with a recap of the last class
2) Teaches today's slated topics
3) Does few examples. He generally solves the problem fully
4) Gives problems to solve in the class
    - He expects immediate answers
    - On doubt he starts by explaining the basics needed for the problem
5) Repeats for other topics through the class

# Maths
- Unlike the other two, my son attends Ahaguru. So, this doesn't have the same personalised experience as the other two classes. This is a mass class of 50+ students.
- The provide prereads to be completed in the form of videos and practice problems. They need to be completed. It is tracked and flagged

## Approach to each class
1) Starts with a revision of what is taught i the videos
2) Then it is fully problem solving
    - The teacher gives a One problem at a time
    - Provides a set time for the student to solve. They can submit the answer in the answer chat bot
    - The teacher then solves the problem, calls out in some cases students who got right and moves to repeat
3) The class will end with an online test, timed
    - Students will have to solve and submit the test online
    - If there are doubts the students should follow up with the mentor
