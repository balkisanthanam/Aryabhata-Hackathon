11.8

A thermodynamic system is taken from an original state to an intermediate state by the linear process shown in Fig. Its volume is then reduced to the original value from E to F by an isobaric process. Calculate the total work done by the gas from D to E to F.

lightbulb

Key Concept: Area Under the Curve

  • Expansion (V↑): Work is positive (+).
    Gas does work on surroundings.
  • Compression (V↓): Work is negative (-).
    Work is done ON the gas.

calculate Full Solution

Step 1: Process D → E Hint: Calculate the area under the trapezoid.
Reveal expand_more

WDE = Area of Trapezoid = ½ × (PD + PE) × (VE - VD)

help Since volume increases from 2.0 to 5.0, the gas is expanding. Expect a positive work value.

= ½ × (600 + 300) × (5.0 - 2.0)
= ½ × 900 × 3.0
= +1350 J

Step 2: Process E → F Isobaric
Hint: Think about volume change direction...
Reveal expand_more

WEF = Area of Rectangle = P × (VF - VE)

warning Note: Volume decreases (5.0 → 2.0), so work is done ON the gas. Expect a negative value.

= 300 × (2.0 - 5.0)
= 300 × (-3.0)
= -900 J

Step 3: Total Work Done Hint: Sum the work from all individual steps.
Reveal expand_more

Wtotal = WDE + WEF

= 1350 J + (-900 J)

Final Result Hint: What is the net work done?
Reveal expand_more

Answer Total Work Done = 450 J
