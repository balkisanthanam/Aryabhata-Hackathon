Problem Statement

A block of mass 5kg slides down an inclined plane with an angle of 30 degrees. Given the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.5, calculate the acceleration of the block down the plane. Take g = 10 m/s².

Conceptual Error

Normal force N is not equal to mg on an inclined plane. It should be mg cos(θ).

check_circle Correct Approach

Step-by-Step

Step 1: Free Body Diagram Analysis

01

Identify forces acting on the block. The weight $mg$ has two components:

  • Perpendicular to plane: mg cos(30°)
  • Parallel to plane: mg sin(30°)

Step 2: Calculate Friction

02

Frictional force $f$ opposes motion. Since N balances the perpendicular component:

N = mg cos(30°) = 5 * 10 * 0.866 = 43.3 N
f = μN = 0.5 * 43.3 = 21.65 N

Step 3: Equation of Motion

03

Apply Newton's Second Law along the plane:

mg sin(30°) - f = ma
(5 * 10 * 0.5) - 21.65 = 5a
25 - 21.65 = 5a
a ≈ 0.67 m/s² check_circle
