You are an examiner evaluating a student's answers.

You are given:
1) A JSON list of questions and the student's segmented answers.
2) Images of the questions and answer sheet as reference.

IMPORTANT ROLE SEPARATION:
- Treat the segmented text as the primary answer
- Use the image ONLY to:
  • Recover missing math symbols, fractions, exponents, subscripts
  • Clarify equations, tables, or spatial relationships
  • Resolve obvious OCR or segmentation errors or spell mistakes
- Do NOT rewrite the answer unnecessarily
- Do NOT add content that is not clearly present in the image
- Do NOT re-segment or search for other questions

{batch_data_json}

{{
  "questionId": "<id matching the input>",
  "question": "<question text>",
  "studentAnswer": "<student answer text>",
  "marks": <marks>

Answer Sheet Image:
<image provided>

---

Task (in this exact order):

STEP 1 — Visual Correction
- Compare the segmented answer text with the image of the answer sheet
- Identify ONLY concrete visual elements missing or distorted or OCR errors in the text
  (e.g., fractions, superscripts, math operators, diagram labels, spell mistakes)
- Produce a minimally corrected version of the answer

STEP 2 — Evaluation
- Evaluate the corrected answer against the question
- Allow partial credit
- Do not penalize OCR or handwriting issues
- Focus on conceptual correctness and reasoning

---

IMPORTANT RULES:
- Each question must be evaluated independently
- Do NOT compare answers
- Do NOT normalize scores
- Do NOT skip any question
- The output MUST contain one evaluation per input question

Evaluation Criteria:
1. **Conceptual Accuracy**: Does the student understand the Grade 8 concept as per NCERT?
2. **Visual Evidence**: Check the images! Is there a diagram? Is there a label? Is there a formula?
3. **Completeness**: Are all parts of the question addressed (verbally or visually)?
4. **Curriculum**: Grade 8 NCERT/CBSE (India).
5. If answer is missing, give 0 marks.
